Terrestrial television is one of the most popular ways to receive TV signals. Here's a detailed guide on how it works and how to set it up:

  1. What is terrestrial television?

    Terrestrial television is a system of transmitting TV signals using radio waves. The signal is broadcast from terrestrial transmitters and received by TV antennas.

  2. Types of terrestrial television:
    • Analog: An older system, now being replaced by digital terrestrial television.
    • Digital (DVB-T): A modern standard offering better picture and sound quality, as well as additional features like EPG (Electronic Program Guide).
  3. How to receive terrestrial television?

    To receive terrestrial television, you need:

    • A TV antenna (external or internal).
    • A TV with a built-in DVB-T tuner or an external DVB-T decoder.
  4. Antenna setup:

    Point the antenna towards the nearest transmitter. You can check the location of transmitters in your area using online maps.

  5. Channel tuning:

    In the TV or decoder menu, select the option for automatic channel search. The device should find the available TV channels.

  6. Reception issues:

    If you have reception problems, check:

    • Whether the antenna is properly positioned.
    • Whether the antenna cable is connected and undamaged.
    • Whether there are signal interferences in your area.

Terrestrial television is a great way to access free TV channels. Thanks to digital technology, you can enjoy high-quality picture and sound.
